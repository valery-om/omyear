## 0:58–1:38 — Show exactly how Maya becomes a book

**Screen:** Open `/try`, prefill synthetic Maya, scroll through the permitted facts
and places, check consent, click generate, and keep several seconds of the progress
state.

> Omyear begins with a person, not a blank prompt. The questionnaire records only what
> Maya chooses to share: birth data, goals, confirmed facts, important places, and the
> intention for her year. Code validates the input, calculates the five systems, and
> creates a registry where every fact and result has a stable source ID. GPT five
> point six receives only that evidence and a strict schema. It writes; it does not
> calculate or invent her biography.

## 1:38–2:22 — Prove that generation delivers the promised experience

**Screen:** Cut from progress to the completed generated result. Its cover must match
the Maya example. Scroll through the opening, the atlas, one month, then open the
source ledger inside the same book.

> When generation is complete, the result is not a different template or a technical
> report. It is the same calm, bilingual book you saw in the example, now created from
> the submitted material. Every interpretation can lead back to a fact Maya shared or
> a value calculated by code. Deterministic checks reject unknown citations, altered
> values, missing chapters, and unsupported places. The source ledger stays visible
> inside the experience, and even a successful run ends with human review—not
> automatic publication.
